Welcome to my video player! I hope you like it. I am definitely not a designer (as will become obvious upon clicking a nav button), but it does the trick. Here I will go through some of the design decisions I made, of what to do and what to avoid, and how it all works.

The short version is that this is an HTML5 video player that plays media fragments, hosted and supported by Angular 4 and Ngrx (a Typescript implementation/variant of Redux). I tried to achieve a balance between a high level of functionality and keep things streamlined/properly organized. As such, there is a decent amount of functionality above and beyond what was requested, but not every feature is there. On the other side, things are broken down into reasonably sized components/reducers, and SCSS is written in a way that avoids fragile posititioning.

However as with all projects, especially short term ones, there is always room for improvement: some components could be broken down further, some SCSS refactored to be even more robust, and so on. I would be happy to discuss the places where you or I feel more could be done. I would love some feedback on this project as I plan to fine-tune it further as time goes on!

While most functionality I feel comes across as relatively obvious, one thing that might not be obvious is that the playlist and clip titles highlight on hover, and are clickable. This is how one starts them playing. Any fragment, whole or otherwise can be played at any time. While nothing occurs at the end of the overall video, clips proceed to play the next video three seconds after each one finishes, in sequential order.

Clips can be created using the "paddles" at the bottom of the video player (in the "privileged" view only), or at the bottom of the playlist section.

Lastly, there are two different views of the application. It is all the same SCSS, HTML and Angular 4 components, however in one view all editing abilities are removed. Creating a user privileges scheme through routing is obviously not a robust practice, but here it serves its purpose. An advanced/better way to do this would be to separate all editing capabilities into components all within a separate module, and only loading that module if the user had the appropriate privileges. This approach might be what is used in the future on this project.

Enjoy!
